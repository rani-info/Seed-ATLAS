Seed Registry

Field indicators for in-situ conservation

  • Current cultivation status: active, occasional, discontinued
  • Farming system: rainfed/irrigated/terrace/forest garden
  • Threats: market pressure, climate stress, seed replacement, loss of knowledge
  • Seed practices: saving, storage methods, exchange networks
  • Verification: self-reported vs observed vs lab-tested

Practical incentive mechanisms

Market-based

Premium pricing, buy-back guarantees, local procurement, branding support.

Non-market

Recognition, community seed banks, input support, training, insurance.

Governance

Benefit-sharing pathways, consent management, safe data sharing.

Equity lens

Who benefits? Women seed-keepers, indigenous communities, smallholders.

Consent-first data sharing

Participation relies on clear consent, controlled sharing levels (public, research-only, restricted), and safeguards for sensitive knowledge. Governance rules define rights, responsibilities, and dispute handling to build trust across farmers, researchers, and market actors.
